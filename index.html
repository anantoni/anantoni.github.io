Investigative journalism / AI criticism / software systems

I report on technology as power, not spectacle.

Based in Greece, I work at the intersection of technology, artificial intelligence, software engineering, business, and digital culture. The throughline is consistent: look past the hype, identify what is actually happening, and explain why it matters.

Journalism Investigations, criticism, and long-form writing anchored in technical detail.
Research PhD work on static analysis, large-scale software systems, and practical precision.
Coverage AI economics, platform power, digital labor, software infrastructure, and governance.

Approach

Technical expertise without marketing varnish.

I write about AI not as a magic trick for investors and executives to applaud on stage, but as a real economic, political, and social force that deserves scrutiny, context, and serious analysis. My work is driven by a strong interest in how technical systems shape power, incentives, institutions, and everyday life.

Research Base

Alongside journalism and long-form writing, I am a PhD researcher in the Department of Informatics and Telecommunications at the University of Athens and a member of the PLaST research group. My academic work on Java static analysis and scalable program analysis gives me a practical foundation for reporting on AI systems, software infrastructure, and the gap between what technology companies promise and what their systems actually deliver.

Editorial Aim

I move between research, criticism, journalism, and technical writing, trying to make complex ideas understandable without flattening them into slogans. A large part of the work is resisting fashionable narratives and replacing them with evidence, analysis, and clarity.

Too much technology coverage is buried under jargon or distorted by public relations. I try to translate difficult subjects without dumbing them down.

Publishing

Across journalism, blogging, commentary, and photography, I have explored technology, gaming, criticism, and culture with the same editorial standard: precision, depth, and perspective.
